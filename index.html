Samit Pal

DST INSPIRE Research Fellow, IIT Indore

I am Samit Pal, a researcher in radio astronomy and cosmology, specializing in the detection and analysis of the HI 21-cm signal from the Epoch of Reionization (EoR). My work focuses on understanding how instrumental effects, calibration errors, and ionospheric distortions affect the recovery of cosmological information from low-frequency radio interferometers such as SKA1-Low. I develop and employ simulation, calibration, and data analysis pipelines using tools like OSKAR, EveryBeam, QuartiCal, and CASA, integrated within Singularity and Docker environments for reproducible large-scale processing. My research combines numerical modeling, statistical inference, and computational optimization to evaluate and mitigate systematic effects in 21-cm cosmology. Broadly, my interests lie at the intersection of cosmology, instrumentation, and data science, with the goal of enhancing the scientific readiness of next-generation radio telescopes and improving our understanding of the early Universe through precision 21-cm measurements.
